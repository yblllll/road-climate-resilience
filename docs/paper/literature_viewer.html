Knowledge Gaps Identified

  • 1. Open Platform Gap (Theme E)
    No study provides a reproducible, open-source platform integrating weather, traffic, and network data for road climate resilience analysis. → Your paper fills this gap.
  • 2. UK-Specific Empirical Gap
    Despite strong UK policy interest, most precipitation-speed studies use non-UK data (US, China, Germany). → Your paper provides UK SRN evidence.
  • 3. Functional Form Consensus
    No consensus on speed-rainfall relationship form (linear, exponential, threshold). → Gamma GLM offers a principled alternative.
  • 4. Multi-Factor Integration
    Only Liu et al. (2026) integrates multiple rainfall impact pathways. Most studies isolate a single mechanism.
  • 5. Temporal Resolution
    Most studies aggregate to hourly/daily. Sub-hourly linking rainfall bursts to real-time speed changes is rare.
  • 6. Recovery Dynamics
    Calvert & Snelder (2018) highlight recovery as essential, but few studies measure post-rainfall speed recovery.
  • 7. Local Road Gap
    Most resilience studies focus on highways. Local road network resilience under rainfall is largely unstudied.
  • 8. Validation Gap
    Cross-validation between simulation predictions and empirical observations under same conditions is almost absent.
